Like thousands of other small business owners, Sarah felt the crushing weight of the 'marketing guilt cycle.' She knew she needed to post to stay relevant, but between managing staff and perfecting her sourdough, the idea of staging photos, writing witty captions, and finding the right hashtags felt like a second full-time job. ## The Silent Toll of the Marketing Gap

Sarah wasn't a 'bad' marketer; she was an expert baker. Yet, the pressure to become an overnight content creator led to burnout. She would go weeks without posting, then panic-post a blurry image that didn't reflect the high quality of her artisanal goods. The disconnect between her passion for baking and her neglected social media presence was more than just an annoyance—it was a tangible drain on her business growth.
